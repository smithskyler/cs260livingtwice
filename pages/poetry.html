Ode to Bo

By Skyler Smith

Through the scorching hot sun walked Sam rather slow,
For all through the night he had lugged his friend Bo.
Flung up o’er his shoulder all dead and quite limp
For his poor friend old Bo had dropped from a blimp.

Then the vultures came circling and asked our mate Sam,
“Could we eat Bo all up, since he is a dead man?”
“Why no!” replied Sam in a terrible fright,
“For Bo is my friend, and I’ll put up a fight!”

“Oh, no need for violence, we’ll be your friends too,”
A vulture replied without hullabaloo,
“But Bo, my good friend, is a weight on your back,
“And does you no good, but to us is a snack.”

“He is rather heavy,” said Sam with a frown,
“I guess you can have the ears from his crown.”
“Oh yes!” cried the vultures; they moved in on Bo,
And ate up the ears, then our Sam called, “Now go!”

“Oh, please,” said the vultures, “Bo’s ears were quite small.
“His feet look alright, ‘sides, they broke from his fall.”
“Alright,” said our Sam, then they ate up Bo’s feet,
Then his hands, then his arms, and even his seat.

“Now you’ve finished him off,” said Sam with a moan,
As the vultures picked the last meat from his bones.
Then all of a sudden Sam stood up and grinned,
For the weight he had carried was dust in the wind.

Unbound

By Skyler Smith

My feet are crossed, my hands are tied,
But I can not, will not, forsake my pride.
The shadows deepen, the pains increase,
My captor's hands push down my knees.

And there between the door or noose
I have but claim a lie is truth
Then win my freedom, for this they said,
"Two liars alive are worth one dead."

I shake my head and raise it high,
A prayer, a whisper, an echoed sigh.
A flash of iron, I fight the club,
But hear no sound from the drips of blood.

My arms are cut, my neck is bruised,
Cold chains about my wrists are used.
They lead me towards my fate, I know
But seeds of freedom do I sow.

A future now my eyes can see,
When there will be no bended knee,
No bloody fists, no broken necks,
No ropes to bind the aching chests.

And now my weakness falls to doom,
But there I see a waking bloom,
When fingers snatch from concrete ground,
These seeds I sow, these seeds unbound.
